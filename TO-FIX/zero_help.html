Is remote support really free? And why do you offer this?
Yes, absolutely. Remote support is always free of charge, although subject to fair use policy. We invite those who can afford to make a small donation so that we can continue to offer this service to many of our older clients who may be on limited pensions and younger families on lower incomes.

We offer free remote support to all of our customers, both new and old, as most problems can be resolved quickly and do not require a visit from a technician; travelling fewer miles is also better for the planet.

About Us

Michael Mansfield

Follow

I have been working in the telecom & data networks industry since 1986, having completed my apprenticeship whilst working at CAMTECH Electronics as part of the design team behind the famous Joint Academic NETwork [JNT X25] (JANet) the forerunner for what we now know as the internet.

JANet developed out of several local and research networks dating back to the 1970s. The JANet system first went live in April 1983, hosting about 50 sites with line speeds of 9.6 kbit/s. In the mid-80s the backbone was upgraded to a 2 Mbit/s backbone with 64 kbit/s access links, and a further upgrade in the early 1990s sped the backbone to 8 Mbit/s and the access links to 2 Mbit/s, making Janet the fastest X.25 network in the world. I started programming at a very early age, working on a wardrobe sized Digital PDP11 made by DEC and then 8088 processor-based personal computers. I have in-depth knowledge of both voice and data transmission protocols I have worked alongside some of the UKs largest network providers, private corporations and government bodies whilst specialising in cyber/ counter-intelligence and network security.

Over the last 35+ years, I have been fortunate enough to work with some pretty special and interesting clients. From providing a full telecom and data solution for Rover & MG Cars (remember them) to installing over 28km of cabling in, above, under and around Pinewood Studios in Ivor Heath (nr London) famous for the great Carry On films and of course the James Bond and Starwars Movies.

During the 1990s and early 00noughties we were the largest supplier and installer of Nortel telephone systems outside of British Telecom and a principle reseller for Avaya, Tennyson and Nortel whilst also retaining Microsoft Gold Partner status.

Now relocated to the South coast of England, Zero42 is quickly becoming the first choice for a multitude of local businesses and individual clients alike.

I hope that you find our NEW website both informative and helpful, and a very warm welcome to Zero42.

Sincerely

Michael Mansfield
